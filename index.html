Zepbound (tirzepatide) produces more weight loss than Wegovy (semaglutide) at maximum doses, 20.9% vs 14.9%. Wegovy has a longer safety record and proven cardiovascular benefits. Zepbound is also about $290/month cheaper at brand pricing.

For pure weight loss, Zepbound has the edge. For heart protection, Wegovy is the proven option.

How These Medications Work

Both medications belong to a class called GLP-1 receptor agonists. They mimic natural hormones in your body that control appetite and blood sugar. When you take these drugs, your stomach empties more slowly. Your brain receives signals that you are full. You feel less hungry throughout the day.

But there is a difference. Wegovy works on one pathway called GLP-1. Zepbound works on two pathways: GLP-1 and GIP. This dual action is why many doctors believe Zepbound produces stronger results. It targets appetite control from two different angles.

Weight Loss Results: What the Research Shows

The weight loss difference between these medications is significant. In the SURMOUNT-1 trial, patients taking the highest Zepbound dose of 15mg lost an average of 20.9% of their body weight over 72 weeks. In the STEP-1 trial, Wegovy patients lost 14.9% over 68 weeks.

For a 250-pound person, that difference means roughly 15 additional pounds with Zepbound. Even at the lowest Zepbound dose (5mg), weight loss was 15.0%, which matches Wegovy's maximum-dose result.

Research & Statistics (Clinical Evidence)
According to data published in the New England Journal of Medicine (2022), the SURMOUNT-1 trial involving 2,539 participants showed that 89% of patients taking tirzepatide achieved at least 5% body weight reduction, compared to 35% in the placebo group. Similarly, the STEP-1 trial published in NEJM (2021) with 1,961 participants demonstrated that 86% of semaglutide users achieved 5% or more weight loss. These trials represent the highest level of clinical evidence, with both studies being randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled phase 3 trials.
Source: NEJM 2022;387:205-216 and NEJM 2021;384:989-1002.

Side Effects: What to Expect

Both medications share very similar side effect profiles because they work through overlapping biological pathways. Nausea is the most common complaint for both, affecting 20 to 44% of Wegovy patients and 24 to 33% of Zepbound patients in clinical trials. Most GI side effects are worst during the first few weeks of a new dose and improve with time.

  • Vomiting: Zepbound 5–12% vs Wegovy 5–24%.
  • Constipation & Diarrhea: Comparable rates.
  • Discontinuation due to side effects: About 4–7% for either medication.

Zepbound has one practical advantage: six dose steps compared to five for Wegovy. That extra granularity allows physicians to make smaller dose increases, which can reduce the severity of GI symptoms during titration.

The SELECT trial proved that semaglutide 2.4mg reduced major adverse cardiovascular events by 20% in patients with obesity and established heart disease. Which Is Better for First-Time Users?

If you are new to GLP-1 medications, both are reasonable starting points. Choose Zepbound if your main goal is maximum weight loss and you want the strongest clinical weight loss data. Choose Wegovy if you have heart disease or high cardiovascular risk, or you prefer a longer safety record. Many patients also consider compounded options when insurance doesn’t cover brand-name drugs.

Can You Switch Between Them?

Switching is straightforward and commonly done. When moving from Wegovy to Zepbound, most providers start at 2.5mg tirzepatide regardless of the previous Wegovy dose. When switching from Zepbound to Wegovy, providers typically start at a lower semaglutide dose and titrate up. There's usually no washout period needed between the two.

The Future of Both Medications

Both Zepbound and Wegovy will face competition from next-generation drugs. Retatrutide (triple-receptor agonist) showed up to 24% weight loss in Phase 2 trials. CagriSema combines semaglutide with an amylin analogue and delivered 22.7% weight loss. Oral versions of both drugs are in development. For now, Zepbound and Wegovy remain the gold standard — FDA-approved, widely prescribed, and available in both brand and compounded forms.
